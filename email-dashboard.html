What's working
"Subject Line A"
Field Event  ·  30.3% open  ·  0.0% CTR
Day-before event reminders with venue specificity reliably drive strong opens — the concreteness makes the email feel imminent and real. Zero CTR is expected for this type; the open itself is the conversion.
"Subject Line B"
Newsletter  ·  17.3% open  ·  15.1% CTR
Strong CTR for a newsletter signals an engaged audience that trusts the send. Datestamp formatting sets expectations and self-selects for interested readers — a reliable pattern for recurring sends.
"Subject Line C"
EBlast  ·  8.2% open  ·  12.0% CTR
Low open but strong CTR — outcome-specific framing resonates with audiences who live the problem daily. Once they open, the value prop converts. A shorter version could unlock more of the list.

What's not landing
"Subject Line D"
Webinar  ·  7.6% open  ·  7.0% CTR
Urgency framing with a hard date underperformed here — the hook reads as alarming rather than helpful to cold prospects. Reframing around a positive outcome may improve opens without sacrificing click intent.
"Subject Line E"
EBlast  ·  8.2% open  ·  13.8% CTR
Curiosity gap format drove average opens but above-average CTR — the subject line works as a filter, attracting the right audience even if total opens are modest. Worth testing with a warmer list segment.
"Subject Line F"
Lifecycle  ·  18.8% open  ·  0.0% CTR
Vague question format generates curiosity opens but no downstream action — the subject line overpromises and the content doesn't convert. Align subject line specificity more tightly with the email's actual CTA.

Recommendations for next week
1
Lead with specificity on event sends. Named venues, dates, and actions ("You're confirmed") consistently outperform generic reminders. Carry this into all event-triggered sends.
2
Audit question-format subject lines. Questions drive opens but rarely CTR. If the goal is clicks, reframe as a benefit statement or outcome declaration.
3
Test shorter versions of high-CTR subjects. The compliance-focused EBlast had a strong CTR despite low opens — a punchier subject line could unlock more of the list without sacrificing conversion quality.
4
Segment EBlasts more tightly. Broad EBlasts are underperforming on opens. Tighter segmentation by role or pain point will improve relevance and open rates across the board.
